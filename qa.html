The Real Questions

Questions worth answering.

The ones that come up most — answered honestly.

01

"How does this actually work?"

I've spent twenty years making things people pay attention to — launching shows, building brands, earning audiences in a world that doesn't hand them out. What I couldn't do was build it myself. I'm not a designer. I'm not a developer. I was the person who could see what needed to exist, but needed a team around me to make it real.

AI changed that. Not by replacing the thinking — I still do all of that — but by giving me a way to execute it. I can take twenty years of experience in brand, audience, and creative strategy and actually build the thing, end to end.

Here's what that looks like in practice. I start by analysing your business — who you're trying to reach, what they need to hear, and whether the way you're showing up is doing that justice. Then I close the gap: a website that does your business justice, built from real strategic thinking, not a template.

But the website isn't the finish line. Through the build process, I create three AI specialists — trained on your brand, your audience, and your visual identity. An Audience Strategist, a Brand Strategist, and a Creative Director. They're yours to talk to whenever you need them. Update your website, build a pitch deck, pressure-test a new direction — no scheduling, no waiting.

The experience is mine. The AI is what lets me deliver it at a scale and a price that wouldn't have been possible two years ago.

02

"Will this just look like AI made it?"

Look at the case studies. That's your answer.

Yes, the three specialists are AI. I'm not hiding that — it's what makes the whole thing possible. But there's a canyon between asking ChatGPT to write your homepage and what this actually is. The difference isn't the technology. It's the context.

Anyone can ask AI to write a homepage. The result sounds like everyone else's homepage. Your specialists are different because they know your business. They know your audience — who they are, what they need, what makes them leave. They know your voice — how you sound at your best, not how a website template sounds. They know your visual language — the palette, the typography, the rhythm that makes your brand feel like yours.

That context comes from the build. Twenty years of brand building experience, encoded into three specialists trained on your business, your audience, and your visual identity. That's the canyon. One gives you everything. The other gives you the right thing.

03

This does. One person with twenty years of experience and an approach that multiplies what one person can do. No office. No account managers. No six-month engagements structured around how agencies bill. The work is the same quality — the structure around it is completely different.

04

"What happens if I don't like what you build?"

Then we haven't finished yet.

The process is built around checkpoints — moments where you see the work, react to it, and steer. You don't get a big reveal at the end and hope for the best. You're shaping it throughout.

And honestly, the checkpoints are where the best work happens. Your gut reaction to a first draft tells me more about what your brand needs than any brief ever could.

05

"What do I actually get? Like, specifically."

A website, yes. But that's the starting point, not the finish line.

The build is where I encode everything — your audience, your positioning, your voice, your visual identity — into three specialists that understand your business. That's what you're really getting: a team trained on you, available whenever you need them.

Once the build is done, those specialists can create whatever comes next — pitch decks, marketing copy, social content, new pages, strategic advice. You access them directly through Claude, the same way you'd message a colleague. I'll walk you through exactly how it works, and I'm always on call if you need a hand.

The website is the foundation layer. Everything else flows from it.

06

Nothing. Not a thing.

After handover, you talk to your team the same way you'd talk to a colleague. Plain language — "I need a slide deck for a pitch next Tuesday" or "can we update the homepage to mention the new service?" — and it happens.

"How is this different from hiring a consultant?"

A consultant gives you a strategy deck and wishes you luck with the execution. This gives you the strategy and the execution — and a team that's still there six months later.

An Audience Strategist who knows who you're talking to. A Brand Strategist who knows what you need to say. A Creative Director who makes sure everything looks like it came from an agency that charges ten times what you paid.

Consultants diagnose. This diagnoses and builds.

08

"This sounds too good — what's the catch?"

Fair question. Here's what this isn't:

It isn't a full-service agency. You won't get a team of twelve people in a Slack channel. You get one person with twenty years of experience and an approach that multiplies what one person can do.

It isn't instant. Good strategy takes a bit of time — we're talking days, not months, but it's not same-day delivery.

And it isn't for everyone. If you want someone to execute a design you've already decided on, you want a production house. If you want someone to tell you what your business actually needs — and then build it — that's what this is.

09

"What happens when my business evolves?"

The whole approach is built for that.

Your team understands your audience, your positioning, your voice. When things change — new service, new market, a pivot — you don't start from scratch. The strategic thinking is built in, not bolted on. You adapt from a position of strength.

And you're never locked in. After the build, you can update your site, create new pages, build documents, get strategic advice — all through your team. The goal is independence, not dependency.
