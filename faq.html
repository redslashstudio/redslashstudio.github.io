The Real Questions

Questions worth answering

The ones that come up most — answered honestly.

01

"Is this actually for someone my size?"

Yes. That's the whole point.

The agencies that do this quality of work charge six figures and want a six-month engagement. The template platforms give you a DIY tool and wish you luck. There's almost nothing in between — and that's the gap I built this to fill.

If you run a business with real ambition but not an enterprise budget, this was built for you. Most of my clients have somewhere between 1 and 30 people. They know their brand isn't working hard enough. They just couldn't previously afford to fix it properly.

02

"I'm not technical — will I actually be able to use this?"

You don't need to be.

After handover, you talk to your brand system the same way you'd talk to a colleague. You describe what you need in plain language — "I need a slide deck for a pitch next Tuesday" or "can we update the homepage to mention the new service?" — and it happens.

No code. No dashboards. No interface you need training on. If you can write an email, you can use this.

03

"What happens if I don't like what you build?"

Then we haven't finished yet.

The process is built around checkpoints — moments where you see the work, react to it, and steer. You don't get a big reveal at the end and hope for the best. You're shaping it throughout.

And honestly, the checkpoints are where the best work happens. Your gut reaction to a first draft tells me more about what your brand needs than any brief ever could.

04

"How is this different from hiring a freelancer?"

A freelancer gives you their time. I give you a team that stays after I leave.

When the project ends, you don't just get files — you get three specialists built from twenty years of broadcast experience.

An Audience Strategist who knows who you're talking to. A Brand Strategist who knows what you need to say. A Creative Director who makes sure everything looks like it came from an agency that charges ten times what you paid.

They know your brand because they were built inside it. And they're yours to keep.

05

"Is this just ChatGPT in a trench coat?"

No. And fair question.

Yes, the three specialists are AI. I'm not hiding that — it's what makes the whole thing possible. But there's a canyon between "ask ChatGPT to write my homepage" and what this actually is.

I spend twenty years learning what makes people pay attention — at the BBC, at Channel 4, across hundreds of broadcasts. Then I spend days encoding that into three specialists trained on your business, your audience, your visual identity. They don't know "brands in general." They know your brand. They know who you're talking to and why.

That's the difference between a search engine and a strategist. One gives you everything. The other gives you the right thing.

You'll notice it in the output. It sounds like you, not like a robot. And when it's not sure, it asks — it doesn't guess.

06

"What do I actually get? Like, specifically."

A website, yes. But also everything that goes with it.

  • Brand strategy
  • Audience profiles
  • Visual identity
  • Copywriting
  • Content tools
  • Marketing assets
  • Social templates
  • Slide decks

The website is the foundation layer — the place where your brand DNA gets encoded. Everything else flows from that. Need a pitch deck six months later? It already knows your brand, your audience, your voice.

07

"Can I change things after, or am I locked in?"

You're never locked in. That's the point of the handover.

After the build, you own everything. You can update your site, create new pages, build documents, get strategic advice — all through the system I set up for you. You don't need to come back to me for every small change.

And if you want to, I'm here. But the goal is independence, not dependency.

08

"This sounds too good — what's the catch?"

Fair question. Here's what this isn't:

It isn't a full-service agency. You won't get a team of twelve people in a Slack channel. You get one person with twenty years of experience and an approach that multiplies what one person can do.

It isn't instant. Good strategy takes a bit of time — we're talking days, not months, but it's not same-day delivery.

And it isn't for everyone. If you want someone to execute a design you've already decided on, you want a production house. If you want someone to tell you what your brand actually needs — and then build it at broadcast quality — that's what this is.

09

"What if my business changes — does everything break?"

No. That's what the strategy layer is for.

Your brand system isn't a static set of files. It understands your audience, your positioning, your voice. When your business evolves — new service, new market, pivot — the system adapts because the strategic thinking is built in, not bolted on.

You're not starting from scratch. You're building on a foundation that was designed to grow with you.
